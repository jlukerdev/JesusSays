## Category 21 — Justice and Mercy

### F-B4001 — REVIEW

- **Teaching ID:** 21.1.1
- **Location:** Justice and Mercy > The Blessing on the Poor and Woe to the Rich
- **Reference(s):** Luke 6:20–21, 26*, Matt 5:3, Matt 11:5, 7–11, Luke 7:22
- **Check:** B4-Cat21 — cross-reference integrity
- **Description:** The primary content of this teaching is the Lukan Beatitudes ("Blessed are the poor — the Kingdom of God is theirs"). However, the references include Matt 11:5, 7–11 and Luke 7:22, which are from a completely different event: Jesus's answer to John the Baptist's disciples ("the blind receive their sight, the lame walk, the poor have good news preached to them"). These are messianic-identity verses, not parallel texts of Luke 6:20–21. While the phrase "poor have good news" appears in both contexts, combining them as cross-references to one "blessed are the poor" teaching conflates two distinct events.
- **Rule citation:** TAXONOMY_STANDARDS.md — references should be parallel accounts or directly supporting texts of the same event/teaching.
- **Proposed action:** Remove Matt 11:5, 7–11 and Luke 7:22 from the reference list of 21.1.1. Those verses already have a standalone teaching at 2.6.3 (Matt 11:4–6*, Luke 7:22–28). Alternatively, verify they were intentionally cross-listed and add a rationale note.
- **Status:** RESOLVED — refs removed
- **Approver:** remove the references as notes
