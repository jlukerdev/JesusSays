### F-B2002 — WARNING

- **Teaching ID:** 2.2.6
- **Location:** The Identity of Jesus Christ > His Relationship with the Father
- **Reference(s):** Luke 9:18
- **Check:** B2-Cat2-Subcategory routing
- **Description:** "Whom say the people that I am? — Jesus questions his disciples while praying alone." Luke 9:18 is the Caesarea Philippi question — Jesus asking who people say he is. Cat 2.2's scope is "unity, mutual knowledge, and mutual indwelling between Father and Son." The "who do men say I am?" question has no Father-relationship content; it is a probe of messianic perception, which falls squarely in Cat 2.6 (Messianic Identity). Additionally, Luke 9:18 already appears as a secondary reference in 2.6.6 ("Asks the disciples at Caesarea Philippi — whom do men say that I the Son of man am?"), making this an unnecessary cross-placement in the wrong subcategory.
- **Rule citation:** CLASSIFICATION_RULES.md §Cat 2.2: "Teachings asserting unity, mutual knowledge, and mutual indwelling between Father and Son." §Cat 2.6: "Teachings affirming fulfillment of Old Testament messianic expectation — the Christ, the Son of David, the one to come."
- **Proposed action:** Move 2.2.6 to Cat 2.6 (Messianic Identity), or delete as redundant with 2.6.6 (which already covers the full Caesarea Philippi pericope with Matt 16:13 / Mark 8:26–27 / Luke 9:18).
- **Status:** OPEN

### F-B2011 — REVIEW

- **Teaching ID:** 8.3.3
- **Location:** The Old Covenant > Moses and the Scriptures Pointing to Jesus
- **Reference(s):** John 7:21–23
- **Check:** B2-Cat8-Subcategory routing (8.3 vs 24.4 or 2.3)
- **Description:** "He defends his Sabbath healing by appealing to the lawful practice of circumcision on the Sabbath." John 7:21–23 is Jesus arguing that if circumcision can override Sabbath, so can healing the whole body. This is a Sabbath-controversy argument using Law logic, not an OT interpretation that points to Jesus as the fulfillment. Cat 8.3's scope is "Teachings where Jesus interprets OT texts or persons as prophetic witnesses to himself (Moses wrote of me, Abraham saw my day, the sign of Jonah)." The circumcision/Sabbath argument does not involve Jesus claiming to be the fulfillment of anything — it is a rabbinic-style legal reasoning in defense of a miracle. Cat 24.4 (Sabbath in Right Perspective) or Cat 2.3 (Divine Authority and Mission) would be more fitting.
- **Rule citation:** CLASSIFICATION_RULES.md §Cat 8.3: "Jesus interprets OT texts or persons as prophetic witnesses to himself." §Cat 24.4 (Sabbath in Right Perspective): "Sabbath healings → Cat 24.4 when the frame is Pharisaical conflict."
- **Proposed action:** Review whether 8.3.3 should move to Cat 24.4 (Sabbath controversy) or Cat 2.3 (authority claim). If it remains in 8.3, provide clarification of how the circumcision argument qualifies as "Scriptures pointing to Jesus."

### F-B2012 — REVIEW

- **Teaching ID:** 10.1.6
- **Location:** Prayer and Communion > The Lord's Prayer — Pattern for Prayer
- **Reference(s):** Mark 9:29*, tagged "prayer"
- **Check:** B2-Cat10-Subcategory routing (10.1 vs 10.2/10.3)
- **Description:** "This kind can come forth by nothing, but by prayer and fasting — some deliverances require sustained, focused prayer." Mark 9:29 is Jesus's explanation to the disciples after the failed exorcism: the condition for this type of power is prayer (and fasting). Cat 10.2 (How to Pray — general instruction on manner and attitude) or Cat 10.3 (Persistence in Prayer) would be more appropriate.
