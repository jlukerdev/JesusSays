4

The Kingdom of God

Matt · Mark · Luke · John

⊞ double-click to focus
Daily Life Application

The Kingdom of God is not a future abstraction but a present reality to be sought, entered, and lived. It reorders priorities, challenges self-sufficiency, and calls for a childlike trust that places God's reign above every other pursuit.

Identity & Worldview Values & Priorities Dependence & Trust
4.1

Nature and Character of the Kingdom

TeachingScriptures
Kingdom of heaven compared to a mustard seed — smallest of seeds grows into a large tree sheltering birds Parable Matt 13:31–32 ·Mark 4:30–32 ·Luke 13:18–19
Kingdom of heaven compared to leaven hidden in three measures of flour — permeates the whole Parable Matt 13:33 ·Luke 13:20–21
Kingdom compared to treasure hidden in a field, and to a merchant who sells everything for one pearl of great value Parable Matt 13:44–46
The kingdom does not come with outward observation — it is in your midst / among you Luke 17:20–21
The kingdom of God is at hand — the time is fulfilled; repent and believe Mark 1:15 ·Matt 4:17

Entering the Kingdom

TeachingScriptures
Entry requires being born again — of water and Spirit; what is born of flesh is flesh; the Spirit moves like the wind John 3:3–8
Unless one becomes like a child, he cannot enter the kingdom Matt 18:3 ·Mark 10:15 ·Luke 18:17
Enter by the narrow gate — broad and easy is the way to destruction; few find the narrow way Matt 7:13–14 ·Luke 13:23–24
Seek first the kingdom and his righteousness — all else will be added; many are called but few are chosen Matt 6:33 ·Matt 22:14 ·Luke 12:31

Prayer and Communion

Matt · Mark · Luke · John

⊞ double-click to focus
Daily Life Application

Prayer is not a religious duty performed for an audience but a direct and personal communion with the Father. Jesus models both its form and its spirit: honest, persistent, forgiving, and rooted in trust rather than technique.

Spiritual Disciplines Dependence & Trust Inner Life & Character

13

Love

Matt · Mark · Luke · John

⊞ double-click to focus
Daily Life Application

Love is not an emotion but a practice — directed first toward God with the whole person, then outward to neighbor, enemy, and fellow disciple alike. The new commandment raises the standard from reciprocity to self-giving sacrifice.

Relationships Community & Mission Identity & Worldview
13.1

Love for God

TeachingScriptures
Love the Lord your God with all your heart, soul, mind, and strength — this is the great and first commandment; all the Law and the Prophets hang on this and love of neighbor Matt 22:37–40 ·Mark 12:30 ·Luke 10:27
13.3

Love for Enemies

TeachingScriptures
Love your enemies, do good to those who hate you, pray for those who persecute you — be merciful as the Father is merciful Matt 5:44 ·Luke 6:27–28
If you love only those who love you, what reward do you have? Even tax collectors do the same; lend expecting nothing in return Matt 5:46–47 ·Luke 6:32–35
Do to others as you would have them do to you — this sums up the Law and the Prophets Matt 7:12 ·Luke 6:31
13.4

The New Commandment

TeachingScriptures
A new commandment: love one another as I have loved you — by this all will know you are his disciples John 13:34–35 ·John 15:12
Greater love has no one than this: to lay down his life for his friends; you are his friends if you do what he commands John 15:13–14
He no longer calls them servants but friends — he has made known everything he heard from the Father John 15:15
22

Discipleship

Matt · Mark · Luke · John · Rev

⊞ double-click to focus
Daily Life Application

Discipleship is not a program but a person — Jesus himself — to be followed at total cost. It demands self-denial, cross-bearing, and the willingness to lose everything the world considers gain. The fruit it produces is the evidence that it is real.

Values & Priorities Identity & Worldview Suffering & Resilience

TeachingScriptures
Follow me, and I will make you fishers of men — come, follow me Matt 4:19 ·Mark 1:17 ·John 1:43
Foxes have holes and birds have nests — the Son of Man has no place to lay his head; let the dead bury their own dead Matt 8:20–22 ·Luke 9:58–60
No one who puts his hand to the plow and looks back is fit for the kingdom Luke 9:62
22.2

Counting the Cost

TeachingScriptures

Anyone who does not renounce family, possessions, and even his own life cannot be a disciple Luke 14:26 ·Luke 14:33
The tower builder and the king going to war — count the cost before you begin Parable Luke 14:28–33
